Help & Reference

Overview

The Scripture Impact Research Survey tool helps field workers capture narrative evidence about how Scripture is accessed, used, and influencing a language community. It emphasizes concrete recent stories over opinions and couples them with a light coding layer for rapid situational classification.

Survey Sections

  • Warm Up & Rapport: Establish context of daily life and language domains.
  • Personal Scripture Experience: Individual encounters, barriers, and motivators.
  • Community & Church Use: Corporate patterns, roles, and domain breadth.
  • Translation Experience (T): Naturalness, clarity, key terms, trust.
  • Access & Engagement (SE): Availability of formats, acquisition, usage pathways.
  • Spiritual Climate (S): Openness, social reactions, hunger or decline.
  • Language Choices (M): Multilingual dynamics and inclusion/exclusion effects.
  • Wrap-Up & Reflection: Prioritized change levers and perceived gaps.

Live Guidance System

When you focus or click a question, the right sidebar updates with contextual micro-prompts:

  • Probe: Deepen or clarify with more story detail.
  • Cue: Remind you to watch for time, actors, domains.
  • Watch: Potential analytic signal or risk (e.g., social cost).
  • Reminder: Process guidance (verbatim capture, neutrality).
  • Note: General section-level emphasis.

If no guidance appears, default to core interviewing: recent concrete events, who/when/where, emotional tone, consequences, language used.

Matrix Coding

The coding assistant helps you translate qualitative narratives into a concise situational profile.

Categories

  • T (Translation): Naturalness, clarity, comprehension, key term semantics, trust.
  • SE (Scripture Engagement): Awareness, access channels, distribution, formats, literacy constraints.
  • S (Spiritual Climate): Openness, appetite, resistance, social reaction patterns.
  • M (Multilingual Dynamics): Language choice patterns, inclusion/exclusion, prestige functions.
  • NI (No Issues): No prominent constraints; equilibrium state.

Use Level (1–4)

  1. Level 1: Rare / isolated vernacular Scripture use.
  2. Level 2: Occasional; limited domains; some enthusiasm.
  3. Level 3: Regular in key domains; emerging normalization.
  4. Level 4: Integrated across personal, corporate, and informal settings.

Primary Code = Category + Use Level (e.g., SE3). Choose the category exerting greatest leverage on change or representing chief constraint/opportunity.

Auto Assess Logic (Simplified)

The algorithm scans saved responses for keyword clusters per category. It suggests flags; you validate. Heuristic precedence prioritizes material transformation issues: T > S > SE > M > NI when equal signals occur. Always override if narrative weight differs.

Interview Ethics & Quality

  • Obtain consent; explain purpose and local data handling.
  • Neutral reinforcement: say "Thank you" not value judgments.
  • Capture near‑term recent events (past week/month) before historical shifts.
  • Prefer verbatim wording; mark emotional or metaphor phrases.
  • Avoid stacking questions; one probe at a time.
  • Respect fatigue; offer pause or stop without penalty.

Data Handling

All entries remain in local browser storage until you export. Clear with the Reset function on shared devices. Exported JSON contains raw narrative + coding meta; remove identifying phrases before wider sharing if necessary.

FAQ

Why lock answers after save? To prevent accidental overwrites while scrolling or probing. Click Edit to refine.

What if Auto Assess seems wrong? Manual judgment prevails. Adjust flags, primary category, or Use Level as needed.

Can I import previous data? Current version supports only on-device capture then export; import may be added later.
